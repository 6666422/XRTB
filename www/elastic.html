Using Elastic Search With RTB4FREE

RTB generates a lot of transactions and there is a great deal of market intelligence available in that data. The issue is how to get at that data. RTB4FREE uses REDIS publish/subscribe channels to transmit its bid requests, bids, wins, pixel loads and click through info. You can easily subscribe to these channels and then process this data yourself.

Out of the box, RTB4FREE works with Elastic Search. There is nothing special you have to do on RTB4FREE to enable this but you do need the RTB4FREE Logstash logger called 'RTB4FREE-Stash-Logger' available here. Of course you also need to set up Elastic Search too. More information about that is available here.

With the RTB4FREE Logstash logger, the following is immediately available to you:

  1. Easy integration between rtb4free and Elasticsearch. Logstash has an input for redis pub-sub, and we make direct use of that.
  2. Centralize search through all log detailed events for debugging or analysis.
  3. Can easily build pie charts, graphs, time-series histograms. Graphs available are:
    1. Total bids.
    2. Total wins.
    3. Bids/wins segmented by campaign (adid).
    4. 4. Bids/wins segmented by creative (impid or image_url).
    5. Bids/wins segmented by publisher (pubid).
    6. Bid price avg/min/max segmented by above (adid, pubid, impid).
    7. Total or avg win costs segmented by above (adid, pubid, impid).
    8. Geo maps of bids/wins segmented by above (adid, pubid, impid).
  4. Scalable. We have worked on an Elasticsearch cluster that was processing 1G transactions/800 GBytes per day of RTB data.
